Samosa

Samosa is a popular Indian snack made with a spiced potato filling wrapped in a crispy pastry shell. These delicious triangles are often served with chutneys and are a favorite at gatherings and street food stalls. Samosas can be baked or fried and are a versatile snack that can be filled with various ingredients.

Recipe:

Ingredients:

  • For the dough:
    • 2 cups all-purpose flour
    • 1/4 cup oil or ghee
    • Salt to taste
    • Water as needed
  • For the filling:
    • 4 medium potatoes (boiled, peeled, and mashed)
    • 1/2 cup green peas (optional)
    • 1 tablespoon oil
    • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
    • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
    • 2 green chilies (chopped)
    • 1 teaspoon ginger (grated)
    • 1 teaspoon garam masala
    • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
    • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
    • Salt to taste
    • Fresh coriander leaves (chopped)
  • Oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Dough:
    • In a large mixing bowl, combine flour, salt, and oil. Mix until the flour resembles breadcrumbs.
    • Add water gradually and knead to form a stiff dough. Cover and let rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Prepare the Filling:
    • Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and mustard seeds, allowing them to splutter.
    • Add green chilies and ginger, saut√© for a minute.
    • Add mashed potatoes, peas, garam masala, coriander powder, cumin powder, and salt. Mix well.
    • Add chopped coriander leaves and mix again. Remove from heat and let cool.
  3. Assemble the Samosas:
    • Divide the dough into small balls. Roll each ball into a thin oval or circle.
    • Cut each circle in half to form two semi-circles.
    • Form a cone shape with each semi-circle, sealing the edges with water.
    • Fill the cone with the potato filling, then seal the top edge with water, pressing to close.
  4. Fry the Samosas:
    • Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat. Add the samosas in batches, frying until golden brown and crispy.
    • Remove and drain on paper towels.
  5. Serve:
    • Serve hot with mint chutney, tamarind chutney, or ketchup.
