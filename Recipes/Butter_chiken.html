Butter Chicken (Murgh Makhani)

Butter Chicken, also known as Murgh Makhani, is a popular Indian dish that is rich, creamy, and flavorful. It features tender pieces of chicken cooked in a mildly spiced tomato-based curry sauce enriched with butter and cream. This dish is often served with naan, rice, or roti.

Recipe:

Ingredients:

  • 500g chicken (boneless, cut into pieces)
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 teaspoons garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1 large onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 cups tomato puree (fresh or canned)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon fenugreek leaves (kasuri methi)
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Chicken:
    • In a large bowl, combine yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, garam masala, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt.
    • Add the chicken pieces to the marinade, ensuring they are well-coated.
    • Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, preferably overnight for better flavor.
  2. Cook the Chicken:
    • In a large pan, heat 1 tablespoon of butter and 1 tablespoon of oil over medium heat.
    • Add the marinated chicken pieces and cook until they are browned on all sides. Remove and set aside.
  3. Prepare the Sauce:
    • In the same pan, add the remaining butter.
    • Saut√© the chopped onions until they are golden brown.
    • Add the tomato puree and cook for about 10-15 minutes, until the sauce thickens and the oil starts to separate from the tomato mixture.
    • Add sugar (if using) and salt to taste.
  4. Combine and Simmer:
    • Add the cooked chicken pieces to the sauce.
    • Stir in the fenugreek leaves (kasuri methi).
    • Simmer for 10-15 minutes on low heat, allowing the flavors to meld.
  5. Finish with Cream:
    • Stir in the heavy cream, and simmer for another 5 minutes.
    • Adjust seasoning as needed.
  6. Garnish and Serve:
    • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
    • Serve hot with naan, rice, or roti.
