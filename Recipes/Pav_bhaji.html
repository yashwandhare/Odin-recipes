PAV BHAJI

Pav Bhaji

Pav Bhaji is a popular street food from Maharashtra consisting of a spicy vegetable mash served with buttered bread rolls. It is enjoyed as a hearty snack or meal and is known for its vibrant flavors and rich texture.

Recipe:

Ingredients:

For the Bhaji:

  • 2 tablespoons oil or butter
  • 1 large onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 large tomato (finely chopped)
  • 1 bell pepper (finely chopped)
  • 1 cup potatoes (boiled and mashed)
  • 1/2 cup carrots (boiled and mashed)
  • 1/2 cup green peas (boiled)
  • 2-3 green chilies (finely chopped, adjust to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 teaspoons pav bhaji masala (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

For the Pav:

  • 8-10 pav (bread rolls)
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • Optional: extra pav bhaji masala for sprinkling on the pav

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Bhaji:
    • Heat oil or butter in a large pan over medium heat.
    • Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
    • Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies, and sauté for a minute.
    • Add tomatoes and cook until they become soft and the oil starts to separate.
    • Add bell pepper, pav bhaji masala, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Cook for a few minutes.
    • Add mashed potatoes, carrots, and green peas. Mix well and cook for another 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally.
    • Adjust the consistency of the bhaji with water if needed. Stir in lemon juice and garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  2. Prepare the Pav:
    • Heat butter in a pan or on a griddle.
    • Cut the pav horizontally and lightly toast them in the butter until golden brown on both sides. Optionally, sprinkle some pav bhaji masala on the pav while toasting.
  3. Serve:
    • Serve the hot bhaji with toasted pav. Garnish with extra coriander leaves, onions, and a slice of lemon.
